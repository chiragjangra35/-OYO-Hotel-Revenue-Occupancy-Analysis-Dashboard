**üîç Key Insights & Analysis**
**1. Revenue Trends: The Weekend Surge**
**Trend:** There is a consistent spike in revenue during weekends.

**Insight:** "Business" category hotels (which make up 61% of revenue) are likely seeing luxury travel.

**Strategy:** Implement Dynamic Pricing‚Äîincrease room rates slightly on weekends to capitalize on high demand, and offer "Business Packages" on weekdays to boost low weekday occupancy.

**2. Location Performance**
**Top Performer:** Mumbai generates the highest revenue (0.66bn), despite not having the highest occupancy. This suggests Mumbai has a higher Average Daily Rate (ADR).

**Area of Concern:** Bangalore has the lowest Average Rating (3.4) compared to Delhi (3.8). This indicates an operational quality issue in Bangalore properties that needs immediate HR/Management intervention.

**3. Booking Channels & Cancellations**
**The Leakage:** The dashboard reveals a 24.84% Cancellation Rate.

**Impact:** Nearly 1 in 4 bookings is cancelled. This disrupts forecasting.

**Recommendation:** Review the cancellation policy. Consider implementing "Non-refundable lower rates" to secure guaranteed revenue.

**üí° Strategic Recommendations**
**Improve Occupancy:** With 57% occupancy, 43% of inventory is perishable (unsold). Launch mid-week flash sales to target the "Standard" and "Premium" room classes.

**Focus on Quality in Bangalore:** Initiate a quality audit for Bangalore properties to improve the 3.4 rating, as reputation directly correlates with future RevPAR.

**Platform Optimization:** Analyze which booking platforms (Filter by Platform) are driving the high cancellation rates and adjust policies for those specific channels.
